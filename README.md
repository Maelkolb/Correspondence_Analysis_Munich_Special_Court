# Interactive Multiple Correspondence Analysis on the individual proceedings of the Munich Special Court (1933-45)

This repository hosts the **interactive** Multiple Correspondence Analysis (MCA) of the individual proceedings before the Munich Special Court (*Sondergericht München*, 1933–1945).

The plot projects the socio-cultural characteristics of defendants — such as nationality, religious and political affiliation, and occupation — together with judicial outcomes — prison, penitentiary, penal camp, fine, death sentence — into a shared geometric space. Categories that lie close together co-occur more strongly; distance from the origin reflects how strongly a category discriminates within the dataset. This makes the structural associations between *who* stood before the court and *how* they were sentenced legible at a glance, revealing patterns of repression rather than individual case dynamics.

## Data provenance

The dataset derives from the 1970s *Archive Inventory* (*Archivinventar*) of roughly 10,000 case files of the Munich Special Court, computationally re-processed (scanning, OCR, parsing, classification) for the study above. The underlying source has been published in print as *Resistance and Persecution in Bavaria 1933–1945* (cf. the Data Availability Statement of the source publication).
